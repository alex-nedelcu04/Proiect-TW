Disney+ Series

Here are the series that take place in the Star Wars universe, aiming either to enrich the Skywalker Saga movies by adding much-needed context or to tell completely original stories that explore how the ever-changing landscape of the galaxy affected the regular members of society.

While The Clone Wars and Rebels were originally Cartoon Network shows, they are now available on Disney+ alongside the newer projects.

Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008-2020)

Description

The Clone Wars is an animated series that showcases the Republic's perspective of the Clone Wars, mainly through the eyes of Anakin Skywalker, his padawan Ahsoka Tano, and his master Obi-Wan Kenobi, but also from the point of view of the clones themselves. The show explores different theaters of war on various planets and civilizations, depicting how the protagonists, from their positions as generals and commanders in the Army, lead their men into battle against villains like Count Dooku, General Grievous, and Darth Maul, who was thought to be dead after the events of Episode I.

It also offers insight into Anakin's mind, his close bonds with his wife, Padmé, and his master, Obi-Wan, as well as his role as a "big brother" to Ahsoka. The events and struggles depicted in the show illustrate his gradual turn to the dark side, by the end of thw seriea his turn being a matter of "when" rather than of "if".

Reception

The show is overwhelmingly praised by fans and critics for its focused storytelling, with every arc having a clear purpose that is well-executed. It serves as a valuable addition in the timeline betweenAttack of the Clones and Revenge of the Sith, as fans gain a better understanding of the war. As a result, The Clone Wars has significantly boosted the popularity of The Prequel Trilogy and of that period of time in general.

Star Wars: Rebels (2014-2018)

Description

Rebels is an animated series that primarily showcases how Ezra Bridger, a young Jedi, and Kanan Jarrus, a more experienced Jedi who served as a Padawan in the Clone Wars, try to survive under the Empire's regime—a period when all Jedi who survived Order 66 were hunted down. It also includes other storylines that intertwine with this main one, such as Darth Maul plotting to kill Obi-Wan Kenobi (and ultimately failing) and Ahsoka being a key figure in the early days of the Rebellion.

Reception

The series was well received, though it often fell short of people's expectations, being seen as a "lesser" show than The Clone Wars, which ran concurrently for a long time. Rebels offered closure for some older characters, introduced new and exciting characters for the future, and continued the story of Ahsoka, a fan favorite, something that was highly appreciated.

Star Wars: The Mandalorian (2019-present)

Description

The Mandalorian is a show that takes place after the fall of the Empire and centers around the life of Din Djarin, a Mandalorian, and Grogu, a member of the same species as Yoda. The series explores not only the transition from the Empire to the New Republic but also the attempt to reunify the Mandalorians under one tribe, after being scattered around the galaxy following the Empire's genocide on their home planet.

Reception

The series was overwhelmingly loved by fans, due to the bond between Din Djarin and Grogu, and because it continues the story of the Mandalorians, which was briefly explored inThe Clone Wars and Rebels.

As of the writing of this article, The Mandalorian & Grogu movie is in the works and planned for release in 2026. It aims to serve as the conclusion to the shared story between The Mandalorian, The Book of Boba Fett, and Ahsoka.

Star Wars: The Bad Batch (2021-2024)

Description

The Bad Batch is an animated series that centers around a group of defective clones whose genetic flaws were specifically exploited to make them enhanced soldiers. The story follows them as they take in and protect a young female clone named Omega. The show serves as a continuation of The Clone Wars, extending beyond the events of Revenge of the Sith. The Bad Batch, as the group is called, were unaffected by the programming change that turned clones against the Jedi, due to their deviations from the standard clone template.

The series also depicts the harsh transition from the Republic to the Empire, including the decommissioning of clones.

Reception

The show was well received because it was the first time the transition to the Empire was properly shown on screen and for the camaraderie and family-like dynamic within the squad.

Star Wars: The Book of Boba Fett (2021-2022)

Description

The Book of Boba Fett is a mini-series (only one season) that explores Boba Fett's return after being presumed dead and his quest to take control of the criminal underworld on Tatooine.

Having first re-emerged in The Mandalorian Season 2, Boba Fett's story is heavily linked to that of Din Djarin, serving as a bridge between the second and third seasons of The Mandalorian. Boba Fett's rise to power after his absence wasn't easy, as he ends up facing off against old enemies, but with the help of The Mandalorian and Grogu, he manages to come out on top.

Reception

The series had mixed reactions. On one hand, people were happy to see fan-favorite Boba Fett again. However, the main critique was that the show wasn't really focused on Boba Fett's story (contrary to what the title suggests), but more on The Mandalorian's. As such, The Book of Boba Fett was labeled by many as an unofficial season of The Mandalorian.

Star Wars: Obi-Wan Kenobi (2022)

Description

Obi-Wan Kenobi is a mini-series that takes place about 10 years after the events of Revenge of the Sith. It presents the life of the Jedi Master with the same name, while in exile on Tatooine, hiding from the Empire while also overseeing Luke to keep him safe from his father finding out about his existence and whereabouts. Leia is also being protected in a similar manner by Bail Organa, who adopted her and raised her as his own.

Obi-Wan is forced to temporarily leave his cover to save Leia after she is kidnapped by the Empire. The series also introduces the Inquisitors into live-action (previously only appearing in Rebels), a group of dark Force users trained by Darth Vader himself with the objective of hunting down Jedi. In his journey to save Leia and subsequently deliver her safely back to Alderaan, Obi-Wan fights these Inquisitors and even Darth Vader himself.

Reception

The series was very well received, being seen as a love letter to fans of the The Prequel Trilogy and The Clone Wars. It masterfully explores the psychological impact Anakin Skywalker's turn into Darth Vader had on Obi-Wan.

The tragedy of the situation is highlighted, as he blames himself for creating this monster and tries to make things right in the least way he can by ensuring that Vader doesn't find out about the twins and turn them into weapons. Plus, the nostalgic aspect of Darth Vader and Obi-Wan Kenobi coming head-to-head again helped the series leave a long-lasting impact on viewers.

Star Wars: Andor (2022-present)

Description

Andor is a series set five years before the events of Rogue One and centers around Cassian Andor. At this time, Cassian is not yet the committed freedom fighter seen in the 2016 movie. He's more self-centered, but the experiences he undergoes transform him into a true Rebel.

The series provides a grounded perspective on life under the Empire's dictatorship. It focuses on everyday people, no Jedi, generals, or politicians, who face the brutal reality of the Empire's rule. Cassian witnesses the horrors inflicted by the Empire in prisons and across society, and these experiences spark the fire of rebellion within him.

Reception

Andor was very well-received for its portrayal of the complexities and tragedies of Imperial rule, taking a more psychological and nuanced approach than any Star Wars media that came before.

Star Wars: Ahsoka (2023-present)

Description

Ahsoka is a series that takes place after the fall of the Empire, during a similar timeframe as The Mandalorian , where Ahsoka first appeared in live-action after previously being part of animated series The Clone Wars and Rebels. The series is a continuation of Rebels, bringing back a number of characters from the show like Ezra Bridger, Sabine Wren, and Grand Admiral Thrawn.

Ahsoka's objective is to find Thrawn and discover what happened to Ezra, who disappeared in the series finale of Rebels as a sacrificial act in their fight against Thrawn. She also takes Sabine under her wing, teaching her the ways of the Jedi.

Reception

The series was well-received by fans because of the live-action debut of many popular characters, especially Thrawn, who has always been an important part of Star Wars lore, appearing in Rebels and in Star Wars books like Heir to the Empire.

Star Wars: The Acolyte (2024)

Description

The Acolyte is a series that takes place about 100 years before the events of The Phantom Menace, during the last days of the High Republic. This period was one of great prosperity for the Jedi, though by its final days, the first signs of a reemergence of the Dark Side began to appear. As such, the series takes a never-before-seen approach, presenting the story from the perspective of the Dark Side.

AA young acolyte takwss on a path that unveils the mysteries surrounding the rise of the Sith. The series also showcases the politics of the time and the early signs of the weaknesses, blindness, and arrogance within the Jedi Order regarding the dangers of a Sith resurgence, traits that were also visible in The Prequel Trilogy and The Clone Wars and ultimately led to the destruction of the Jedi Order at the hands of the Sith.

Reception

The series received mostly negative reviews. Even though fans appreciated their first glimpse of the High Republic era, which had only been touched upon in books and older games, the casting choices and narrative decisions left much to be desired, while real-life politics ended up stirring far more controversy and negativity than anticipated.
